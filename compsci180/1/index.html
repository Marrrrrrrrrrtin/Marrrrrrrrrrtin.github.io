Part 1 — Selfie: The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

Take a close-up selfie ("wrong"), then step back several feet and zoom to match face size ("right"). Compare distortion.

Second photo is much more cute! Her name is Emerald. The camera is like a point, which receives light from a fan-shaped area. When the camera is really close, the stretch will be really significant, making it wired. However, when the camera is further, the light is more similar to parallel, making it more beautiful and natural, as it’s almost the same as the image received by our eyes.

Part 2 — Architectural Perspective Compression

Pick a long path (street/corridor). First: stand far and zoom in. Second: walk up so the subject is the same size but use a wider lens/no zoom.

The first one is from far distance, which seems more similar to what it actually looks like, and the second one engages some stretch as it was shot closer. Since the object is larger which makes the relatively distance different, even if the distances of 'closer version’ in Part II is further than that of ‘further version’ in Part I, the stretch effect is more obvious.

Part 3 — The Dolly Zoom

Move the camera backward while zooming in (or forward while zooming out) so your subject stays the same size.
